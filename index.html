Advice from instructor

Cadet College!

Every student's dream place. Hundreds of students will fight against the limited seats of 12 cadet colleges. Mathematics is a big part of this exam. Many people are afraid of mathematics. Again, many people think that it is necessary to do maths up to class ten in the cadet college admission test! That is not the case at all. Everyone should first understand and finish the 6th-grade math book. Now you should find out from the syllabus which of these topics are also in a higher category. For example, algebra formulas are also in 8th-grade books. It is necessary to know the formulas for squares and cubes, but it is not necessary to do all the arithmetic in a 7th-grade book. Similarly, the formulas of measurement and measurement, how to take from one unit to another, and the definition of geometry - these basic things need to be very well understood. At the same time the unitary rules of arithmetic, interest calculation, profit-loss, ratio, and percentage- these problems should be solved. Time is a big challenge in cadet college admission tests, and this challenge seems to be the most in mathematics. So, in some cases, you have to learn to do the math in a nutshell. However, if you do not learn the basic rules, you can do the opposite. Cadets are not allowed to use calculators in college admission tests. There will be no big calculation that requires a calculator. So you should practice arithmetic without a calculator in advance.

Finally good luck to everyone.
